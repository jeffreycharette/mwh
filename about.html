About Mary Wescott Higgins

Political Philosophy

Mary describes herself as a moderate Democrat. She believes government should be smart, efficient, and accessible to everyone. Advocacy for people who can not speak for themselves is her passion. Her political views have been shaped by her experience in working with and for citizens who are often overlooked in the political process.

I’m running not because I have any great ambition for a political career ... I’m actually quite happy to be out of the limelight! But what’s happening in our rivers, and in our government, is a tragedy. I’m more afraid of what will happen if I don’t run -- if nobody steps up to make a change. I’m capable, and I’m passionate about this cause, these people. That’s why I’m running -- for them.

Biography

Early Life

Mary Louise Wescott was born on October 3, 1956 in Castro Valley, California. She was adopted at birth by Warren and Margaret Wescott and raised in Oakland and Lafayette, California. Her parents met in church on Easter Sunday while her father was stationed at Camp Lejuene, North Carolina. They dated by mail throughout the war and were married on Thanksgiving Day, 1945. Mary's mother was a homemaker. Her father was a Marine during World War II and fought in the Battle of Iwo Jima. After the war, he went to work for Sherwin Williams and retired in 1984 as the Vice President of National Sales.

Education

Mary attended Mount Union College in Alliance, Ohio, where she received a BA in Sociology in 1978.

Family

Mary has been married to Tony Polito since 2001. She has three adult sons. Her son Kelly and his wife Monique, live in Kaneohe, Hawaii with their three children. Patrick and his wife Channi live in St.Augustine, Florida and her son Robert lives in Indian Harbor Beach, Florida.

Mary moved to Port Salerno from Hollywood, Florida with her three children in 1996. A single mom, she was attracted to the beauty, safety, and excellent school system offered by Martin County. She was able to purchase her Port Salerno house by taking advantage of the SHIP loan program. Mary still lives in that home today.

Work Experience

Mary began her social work career in 1990, working for ARC of South Florida where she ran the guardianship program and was later promoted to Director of Advocacy for people with developmental disabilities. Since moving to Martin County, she has done case management for people with HIV/AIDS, mental illness and developmental disabilities. In 2002, working in conjunction with the Florida Department of Revenue, Mary was part of a team that received a Work Force Development Award for their efforts in securing job placement for non-custodial parents who were not paying child support.

Mary is currently an independent contractor working for the Florida Agency for Persons with Disabilities. She is a Waiver Support Coordinator, providing intensive case management for adults and children with developmental disabilities. These disabilities include intellectual disabilities, cerebral palsy, autism and Prader-Willi syndrome. Mary has 24 years of experience with Medicaid, Medicare and Social Security programs and undergoes a level 2 background screening every five years through the Florida Department of Law Enforcement. She is a registered Medicaid provider and has extensive knowledge of and experience with the complex interactions of various state and federal agencies.
