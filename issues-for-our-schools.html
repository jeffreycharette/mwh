For Our Schools

Education

Mary moved to Port Salerno with three school-age children in 1986. Martin County's excellent school system was one of the main reasons she chose to relocate here. Martin County still has excellent schools, ranking 3rd of Florida's 67 county school systems.

For over a decade, there has been a movement to give corporations and other private entities more say in how our schools are run. Our schools are being starved of funds on the one hand and made a source of private profits on the other. There has been a movement to institute high-stakes testing and also a movement to blame teachers when promised improvements fail to materialize.

The most recent battle in this war against public schools, teachers, and teachers' unions was the parent trigger bill which failed to pass in each of the last two legislative sessions. This past year, two groups from outside of the state, Parent Revolution and Michelle Rhee's Students First, were brought in to create the appearance of grassroots support despite the opposition to the bill of every prominent parents group in Florida. The attempt ended with the embarrassing revelation that a significant number of the 1300 petition signatures gathered by Rhee's group were fraudulent.

The educational "reform" movement is not really about helping parents or students or improving educational outcomes as much as it's about improving the bottom lines of for-profit companies. In 2012, charter school and other private education interests donated more than $2 million to state political campaigns.

Florida's teachers currently rank 47th in the nation in pay. It's time to stop making our teachers scapegoats and treat them with the respect they have earned and deserve. Teachers are not the problem, they are part of the solution.
